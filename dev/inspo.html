Section 4.2: Structural Compliance

4.2.1 General Requirements. All structures designated as Class A must adhere to the seismic safety standards outlined in Chapter 3. Compliance is mandatory for all new constructions effective immediately upon the ratification of this document. Existing structures are granted a grace period to retrofit their foundations and load-bearing walls.

4.2.2 Retrofitting Timeline. The timeline for retrofitting is determined by the occupancy category and the seismic zone. For Class A structures located in Zone 1 and Zone 2, the owner must submit a compliance plan within 3 months of notification.

Key Regulation

The mandatory physical compliance period for completing all retrofitting works for Class A structures is strictly set to 12 months from the date of plan approval. Failure to meet this deadline will result in penalties described in Section 9.

4.2.3 Exceptions. Exceptions to the timeline may be granted by the municipal board only in cases of force majeure or documented supply chain disruptions affecting critical materials (steel, concrete).

4.3 Inspection Protocols

Inspections must be carried out by a certified structural engineer. The initial inspection report serves as the baseline for the retrofitting plan. A mid-term inspection is required at the 6-month mark to ensure progress aligns with the submitted schedule.

Upon completion, a final certificate of compliance will be issued. This certificate must be displayed in the main lobby of the structure.
